About Us

Image by wirestock on Freepik

Welcome to the NSERC-Alliance Grant “Next-Generation Wood Construction” research program website.

We are focused on advancing the use of wood in tall and large buildings. Our program is a joint initiative of the Canadian wood industry, government agencies, and the construction sector, aimed at overcoming technical and non-technical challenges that hinder the expansion of wood construction.

The growing interest in using wood in tall and large buildings presents tremendous economic growth opportunities for the Canadian wood industry, which is one of the major pillars of the Canadian economy. However, there are gaps in design methodologies, performance data, environmental impact quantification, and a lack of highly qualified personnel to meet the needs of the expanded use of wood.

Our multi-disciplinary research program aims to address these challenges by conducting research in various areas such as structural safety, serviceability, fire safety, energy efficiency, durability, construction and sustainability of wood buildings. Our team of experts, consisting of 31 faculty members from 14 Canadian universities and 20 scientists and professionals from 12 government and industry partners, will supervise over 50 highly qualified personnel (HQP) trainees to conduct the research activities.

Through our research, we hope to facilitate the increased use of wood products in construction, leading to socio-economic benefits for Canada.

If you have any questions or would like to learn more about our program, please do not hesitate to contact us. Thank you for visiting our homepage.

Technical Information

Our research program is multi-disciplinary and encompasses a wide range of topics, including:

  • Theme 1 - Structural and Serviceability Performance:

    Our research program focuses on the development of design methodologies that ensure the structural safety and serviceability of tall and large wood buildings. To achieve this, we will develop advanced computational tools that can analyze the performance of wood structures under different loads and environmental conditions. We will also develop tools to assess the serviceability of wood structures under different environmental conditions and loads. Through our research, we aim to facilitate the increased use of wood products in construction, leading to socio-economic benefits for Canada. Our research projects under this theme include:
    • Seismic performance of balloon MT walls and cores (T. Yang )
    • Seismic performance of innovative braced timber frames (A. Salenikovich)
    • Performance of MT flat-plate systems under out-of-plane loading (T. Tannert)
    • In-plane performance of mass timber diaphragms (J. Erochko)
    • Estimation of building periods and accelerations due to wind and seismic loads (G. Doudak)
    • Stiffness properties of timber connections (Y. H. Chui)
    • Performance of wood-frame buildings on podiums (L. TobberTBD)
    • Innovative high-capacity shear walls for wood-frame buildings (L. Zhou)
    • Resilient timber buildings - structural performance (S. Tesfamariam)
    • Reliability-based design of MT components and systems (F. Lam)
    • Performance-based design of wood-based systems (C. Loss)
    • Development of generalized vibration serviceability design method for MT floors (L. Zhou)
    • Acoustic design of MT assemblies (S. Ménard)

  • Theme 2 - Fire safety:

    We aim to develop fire safety guidelines for tall and large wood buildings. Our research will focus on developing advanced computational tools to analyze the behavior of wood structures under fire conditions and developing design methodologies that can enhance the fire resistance of wood buildings. Our research projects under this theme include:
    • Determination of a risk-based framework and performance parameters for safety design (E. Weckman and J. Gales)
    • Compartment fire dynamics in timber structures under differing ventilation scenarios (E. Weckman and C. Dagenais)
    • Design fires and charring rates for mass timber analysis (D. Lacroix and C. Gales)
    • Fire performance of connections (S. Salem and A. Salenikovich)
    • Encapsulation of timber elements (H. Hajiloo and S. Salem)
    • Fire Design of Prefabricated and Modular Buildings (C. Dageneis)

  • Theme 3 - Building Envelope and Energy Performance:

    We aim to develop energy-efficient design methodologies for tall and large wood buildings. Our research will focus on developing advanced computational tools to analyze the energy performance of wood structures and developing design methodologies that can improve the energy efficiency of wood buildings. Our research projects under this theme include:
    • Hygrothermal performance of mass timber envelope systems (H. Ge and C. Cruickshank)
    • Field measurements of the moisture and thermal mass effects of MT (Y. Chen and H. Ge)
    • Field monitoring of whole building performance of mass timber buildings (P. Mukhopadhyaya and L. Gosselin)
    • Optimization of building envelope design for net-zero energy and climate resilient wood-frame buildings under projected future climates (Y. Chen)
    • Mitigating overheating risk in wood buildings under current and future climates (H. Ge and L. Wang)
    • Improved resilience against water damage caused by indoor leakage (P. Mukhopadhyaya)
    • Energy-efficient ventilation design for healthy indoor environment in wood buildings (L. Wang)
    • Prefabricated, wood-based multi-function envelope systems for energy retrofitting (C. Cruickshank)
    • Development of digital technology to facilitate long-term field monitoring (L. Gosselin)

  • Theme 4 - Sustainable Construction Technologies and Practices:

    We aim to develop design methodologies that can ensure the durability and sustainability of tall and large wood buildings. Our research will focus on developing tools to assess the long-term performance of wood structures under different environmental conditions and developing design methodologies that can enhance the durability and sustainability of wood buildings. Our research projects under this theme include:
    • Just-in-time (JIT) production and logistics management model for off-site construction (N. Lehoux)
    • Identification and analysis of the barriers to development of off-site construction (N. Lehoux)
    • Design of prefabricated wood buildings using BIM concept. (Md. Al-Hussein)
    • Optimization of the value chain for off-site wood construction in a circular economy (N. Lehoux)
    • Development of a novel acoustic design methodology for wood construction by integrating LCA (S. Menard)
    • LCA of material and utility flows from forest to wood buildings in Canada. (Q. Tu and B. Amor)
    • Low life cycle energy and environmental impact of MT on urban development (B. Amor)
    • Development of biogenic carbon life cycle inventory and life cycle impact assessment for long-lived harvested wood building products (B. Amor and E. Thiffault)
    • Reduction of environmental footprint of wood buildings through performance-based design (PBD) (P. Blanchet and T. Yang)
    • Carbon-based optimization of wood building designs under projected future climates (B. Amor)
